I work at the intersection of computational linguistics, legal language, and German studies, with a focus on diachronic semantics, discourse analysis, and NLP-based legal reasoning.

Current projects include semantic drift analysis of key legal concepts in German corpora (e.g., Rechtsstaat, ius, Liebe) using contextualized embeddings.

Selected Projects

  • Semantic Drift of “Rechtsstaat”
    Diachronic embedding clustering across Bundestag corpora (1949–).
  • Dynamic Word Field of “Liebe”
    BERT-based contextual vector comparison in Der Spiegel (1947–2024).
  • Legal Reasoning Quantification
    NLP analysis of argumentative structures in German court judgments.
